Create your first test | Tutorial

Watch this short video to learn how to get started with . We will show you how to create your first project and model your first test on the fly. Detailed steps follow.

For your convenience, we have also listed the steps for the main procedures below the video.

To create your first project:

  1. Log into .

  2. On the landing page, under Codeless Automation, click BUILD WEB TEST.

  3. Do one of the following:

    • If this is your first project, on the Welcome screen, enter a project name, test name, and the URL to test.

    • If a project already exists, in the My projects view, click ADD NEW PROJECT, and then enter a project name, test name, and the URL to test.

  4. (Optional) Specify the platform on which the test will run.

  5. Click START BUILDING TEST.

    The project opens on the interactive modeling page, with the steps shown in the panel on the left and a live browser on the right. By default, a new project consists of an initialization step, a step that navigates to a specific URL, and a finalizing step. The test pauses after navigating to the specified URL. Now you can modify your test.

To modify your first test:

  1. On the interactive modeling page, to add a new step to the test, click an element on the page in the browser, such as a field or button. The element is identified with a blue border.

  2. On the toolbar that displays, click the required action, such as a click, text validation, element validation, or hover action, or also click the menu icon (three dots) to view more options and then click the required action. In the panel on the left, the action gets added as a new step.

  3. To add additional steps, in the left panel, click Add step.

  4. To finalize the test, click the orange stop sign on the last step to release the breakpoint.
